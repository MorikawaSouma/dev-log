# Automation Maintenance Policy
> Clear boundaries for automated operations to ensure content quality and security.

## 🟢 Allowed Automated Operations
Content that can be automatically written without manual confirmation:
1. **Daily log draft**: Automatically record daily work content, tool usage, problem solving processes
2. **Learning note draft**: Automatically organize technical knowledge points and learning summaries
3. **Resource collection draft**: Automatically collect browsed high-quality technical resources, articles, and open source projects
4. **Experiment record draft**: Automatically record experimental processes, test results, and data
5. **Weekly report first draft**: Automatically summarize weekly work content and generate weekly report framework

## 🟡 Manual Confirmation Required
Content that must be reviewed and confirmed by humans before submission:
1. **External evaluation**: Any evaluation of other people's projects, technologies, or products
2. **Project conclusion**: Final conclusions of experiments, technical selection conclusions, and experience summaries
3. **Recommended content**: Resource recommendations, tool recommendations, and tutorial recommendations
4. **Cross-project judgments**: Any content involving evaluation or judgment of third-party projects
5. **Public endorsement**: Any content that may be interpreted as personal endorsement or recommendation
6. **Sensitive content**: Any content involving business secrets, personal privacy, or sensitive topics

## 🔴 Prohibited Automated Operations
Any behavior that violates GitHub community rules or forges reputation is strictly prohibited:
1. **Batch operation**: Automatic batch follow, star, watch other users or projects
2. **Automatic interaction**: Automatic comments on other people's projects, automatic opening of issues or PRs
3. **Forged interaction**: Any behavior of forging comments, issues, PRs, or other interactive records
4. **Forged reputation**: Any behavior of forging Stars, Forks, or other reputation indicators
5. **Spam content**: Automatic generation and submission of meaningless junk content or placeholder text
6. **Cross-repository operations**: Any operation that modifies other repositories without explicit authorization

## 🤖 Automated Operation Specifications
1. **Content quality**: Automatically generated content must be true and valid, no fictional or forged content
2. **Submission frequency**: No more than 5 commits per day, avoid centralized large-scale submission
3. **Submission time**: Automated operations are mainly performed during working hours (9:00-21:00)
4. **Draft mark**: Automatically generated drafts must be clearly marked as drafts to distinguish them from manually confirmed content
5. **Regular review**: Automatically generated content must be manually reviewed and cleaned up regularly

## ⚠️ Violation Handling
If the automated operation violates the above rules:
1. Immediately stop the corresponding automated task
2. Roll back the problematic submission
3. Adjust the automation rules to avoid recurrence
4. Manually review all content generated during the abnormal period
